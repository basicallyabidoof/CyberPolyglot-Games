What Is This Game?

Multilingual OSINT Puzzles is a CTF-style (Capture the Flag) open-source intelligence challenge platform built for the global cybersecurity community. Every puzzle is grounded in real-world OSINT tradecraft — researching people, domains, images, social media, and more — but the twist is that clues and targets are drawn from a wide range of languages and cultural contexts.

Whether you read Arabic, Spanish, Mandarin, or only English, you will encounter evidence that demands creative thinking beyond your native language. That is the point: modern threats don't stay in one language, and neither should your skills.

How to Reach the Game

The game is hosted at:

https://osint.cyberpolyglots.org

No account registration required. Enter any agent handle (your display name) on the landing screen to begin.

How to Play

  1. Choose an agent handle. This is your identifier on the leaderboard. Pick something memorable.
  2. Browse the puzzle board. Puzzles are displayed as a grid. You can filter by category, difficulty, or search by keyword. Toggle the "hide solved" switch to keep your board tidy as you progress.
  3. Open a puzzle. Each puzzle card shows the category, language, difficulty, and point value. Read the description carefully — it contains your mission briefing.
  4. Investigate. Use publicly available tools and resources to find the answer. No hacking, no private data — pure OSINT.
  5. Submit a flag. All answers are formatted as flag{your_answer}. Type it into the submission field and confirm.
  6. Use clues sparingly. Each puzzle may have clues available. Revealing a clue reduces your point reward for that puzzle, so try to go flag-first.

Difficulty Levels

Easy Medium Hard Insane

  • Easy — Good starting point. Fundamental OSINT techniques, minimal language barrier.
  • Medium — Requires combining multiple sources or working with non-English content.
  • Hard — Deep dives, obscure sources, multi-step inference chains.
  • Insane — Maximum difficulty. Expect to be humbled.

Scoring Rules

  • Each puzzle has a fixed point value based on its difficulty.
  • Revealing a clue reduces the reward for that puzzle — the more clues used, the fewer points you earn.
  • First-blood bonuses may apply for the first solver of a puzzle.
  • Your total score and solve count are both tracked on the leaderboard.
  • There is no time penalty — take as long as you need.

Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks agents by solve count first, then by total score. It is visible to all players at any time from the puzzle board — click the leaderboard button in the top bar.

Community & Code of Conduct

  • Do not share flags publicly — it spoils the challenge for others.
  • All OSINT must use only publicly available information.
  • Play fair: no scripted brute-forcing of the flag submission system.
  • This game is part of the CyberPolyglots mission to build a global, multilingual infosec community. Respect all languages and cultures represented in the puzzles.
