What Is This Game?

Phish Catchers: Localized Edition is a team-based phishing detection game that trains you to recognize social engineering attacks drawn from cultures and languages around the world. You will read emails and SMS messages — some legitimate, some malicious — and decide whether each one is a phishing attempt or the real thing.

The catch: phishing tactics look different depending on where the attacker is targeting. A Nigerian prince scam reads nothing like a fake WeChat security alert or a spoofed Brazilian bank SMS. This game teaches you to read the cultural subtext, not just the technical red flags.

How to Reach the Game

The game is hosted at:

https://lingua.cyberpolyglots.org

No registration required. Enter a team name to join an existing team or create a new one. Teammates who enter the same team name will share a pooled score.

How to Play

  1. Enter a team name. You can play solo (just use a unique name) or coordinate with teammates to use the same name and build a shared score.
  2. Read each message carefully. You will see either a full email interface (with From, To, Subject, Date, and body) or a simulated smartphone SMS conversation. Examine everything — sender address, domain, tone, grammar, urgency, and cultural context.
  3. Make your call. Click Legitimate or Phishing for each message. You cannot skip.
  4. Identify the red flag (bonus round). After a correct answer, a follow-up modal asks you to identify the single biggest red flag in the message. Select the correct one for a +50 point bonus.
  5. Keep your streak alive. Consecutive correct answers multiply your score. Breaking a streak resets the multiplier.
  6. Save and resume. Use the Save & Quit button at any time. On your next visit, the game will ask whether you want to resume your previous session or start fresh.

Red Flags to Watch For

🔗
Fake domains Lookalike URLs, extra subdomains, subtle misspellings (paypa1.com, amaz0n-security.net).
✍
ESL grammar errors Awkward phrasing, incorrect articles, or machine-translated cadence that doesn't match a professional sender.
⚡
Urgency & pressure "Your account will be closed in 24 hours", "Act now or lose access", manufactured deadlines.
🌎
Cultural tells Incorrect honorifics, culturally inappropriate phrasing, holidays or events that don't match the supposed sender's region.

Scoring Rules

Correct classification (Legit or Phishing) Base points
Correct red flag identification (bonus) +50 pts
Consecutive correct streak x multiplier
Incorrect classification 0 pts, streak resets

Team scores are the sum of all teammates' individual scores. Coordinate with your team to maximize multiplier streaks.

Level Structure

There are 16 levels in total. Each level presents one email or SMS. The game tracks your progress with a progress bar (Level X / 16). Difficulty and geographic diversity increase as you advance — early levels present obvious phishing attempts while later levels require careful cultural analysis to distinguish from legitimate messages.

Team & Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks teams by total pooled score. Individual player contributions are summed, so larger coordinated teams can outpace solo players — but every member needs to perform to keep the streak multiplier alive. The leaderboard is visible from the main game screen at any time.

Tips for New Players

  • Read the sender address, not just the display name — attackers often set a friendly display name while the actual address is nonsense.
  • Hover over or carefully read any links in the email body before deciding.
  • Legitimate organizations rarely demand immediate action via email or SMS.
  • If a message is in a language you don't recognize, that itself is a signal — think about who the sender claims to be and whether the language makes sense.
  • The streak multiplier is worth protecting. When in doubt, slow down rather than guessing.
